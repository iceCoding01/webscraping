Good Tidings Community Service Scholarship
Amount:
$10,000
Deadline:
November 3, 2025

The Good Tidings Foundation, a non-profit 501(c)(3) children's charity, is dedicated to uplifting marginalized children in the Bay Area. Our mission is to create spaces for athletics, artistic activities, education, and wonder, offering opportunities for growth that transcend financial status, ethnic origin, or religion. The $10,000 scholarship check will be dispersed evenly over the course of 4 years. Winners will be notified by November 25th. A total of 10 applicants will be accepted to receive this scholarship. Scholarship Details $10,000 award, dispersed evenly over 4 years. Number of Awardees: 10. Eligibility Criteria High school senior. Must be a resident of one of the following California counties: Alameda Contra Costa Lake Marin Monterey Napa Sacramento San Benito San Francisco San Joaquin San Mateo Santa Cruz Santa Clara Solano Sonoma Stanislaus Yolo Must volunteer a minimum of 1 shift (approximately 2-4 hours) for the Good Tidings' Winter Wonderland Toy Giveaway Program in Burlingame, CA (December 6-21). Selection Criteria Scholarships are awarded based on service and financial need. A letter of recommendation is required and must be submitted by the application deadline. Application Process Ensure you meet the eligibility criteria for residency, education plans, and volunteer requirements. Complete the scholarship application by the deadline. Submit a letter of recommendation along with your application. Be prepared to volunteer at least one shift for the Winter Wonderland Toy Giveaway Program in December. Good Tidings encourages and supports the growth of marginalized children in Northern California by creating environments for athletics, the arts, education and wonder. We believe that every child deserves the access and the means to pursue their dreams. The Good Tidings Foundation aims to provide opportunities that otherwise would be unavailable to kids regardless of their financial status, ethnic origin, or religion. Our programs allow children to play, create, and dream.

Regeneron Science Talent Search Scholarship
Amount:
$250,000
Deadline:
November 6, 2025

Each year, nearly 1,900 students enter the Regeneron STS, submitting original research in critically important scientific fields of study. Unique among high school competitions, the Regeneron STS focuses on identifying, inspiring, and engaging the most promising scientists among the nation’s high school seniors. All Regeneron STS entrants must follow the official rules of entry, which outline eligibility requirements, ethics statements, and rules for scientific research. Scholarship Details Focus: Identifying and inspiring future leaders in the field of science Total Annual Awards: $3.1 million First Place Prize: $250,000, the largest academic prize available to a U.S. high school student Eligibility Criteria Applicants must be in their final year of high school. They should be completing the necessary high school courses for college applications. Applicants must not have graduated from high school before the Regeneron STS application deadline. Previous participation in any prior STS is not allowed. Application Process Prepare your application for the Regeneron Science Talent Search, including your original research in scientific fields. Follow the official rules of entry outlined in the application guidelines. Submit all required materials and application before the deadline. If you are a high school senior with a passion for scientific research and a commitment to pushing the boundaries of knowledge, the Regeneron Science Talent Search is a remarkable opportunity.

VFW Voice of Democracy Competition
Amount:
$35,000
Deadline:
October 31, 2025

Since 1947, the Veterans of Foreign Wars (VFW) has dedicated itself to fostering patriotism and nurturing the potential of the future generation. The Voice of Democracy audio-essay program, initiated by the VFW, provides a remarkable platform for high school students (grades 9-12) to express their thoughts on democratic and patriotic themes through recorded essays. Scholarship Details Provides a unique platform for self-expression on democratic and patriotic themes. Awards over $2 million in educational scholarships and incentives annually. National first-place winner receives a $35,000 scholarship sent directly to their American university, college, or vocational/technical school. Additional national scholarships range from $1,000 to $21,000. Eligibility Criteria Open to high school students in grades 9-12. Must be a US citizen or permanent resident. Must be planning to attend college following their high school graduation. Application Process Read the rules and eligibility requirements. Download and complete the Voice of Democracy entry form. Submit entries to a sponsoring local VFW Post by the scholarship deadline. A complete list of other national scholarships range from $1,000-$21,000, and the first place winner from each VFW Department (state) wins at least a scholarship of $1,000. Students interested in participating must submit their audio essay prepared on this year's theme, with a completed entry form, to their local participating VFW Post.

American Indian Services Scholarship
Amount:
$2,500
Deadline:
November 1, 2025

The AIS Scholarship program is to help undergraduate Native American/Alaska Native students with financial support to attend a college or university, maintain enrollment, and graduate. Scholarship Awards are based on need for the term the student is applying for and are allocated directly to the school for the student. Scholarship Details Scholarship Awards are based on up to one-half of tuition costs and the student's financial need for the term they are applying for. The scholarship amount is allocated directly to the school on behalf of the student, reducing the financial burden on the recipient. Applicants must have their own email account to apply for this scholarship. Eligibility Criteria Applicants must be enrolled or plan to enroll as matriculated students in a university, college, junior college, or technical school located in the United States. This scholarship is open to incoming freshmen. Students must enroll in an accredited school. It's important to verify the accreditation status of your chosen school. Applicants must be enrolled members of a U.S. Federally Recognized American Indian or Alaska Native Tribe. Alternatively, they can be descendants of base roll members. Students must be undergraduates with no more than 150 semester credits or 210 quarter credits. This scholarship is for those pursuing their first bachelor's degree; graduate studies are not currently eligible. Applicants can have full-time or part-time status, excluding non-credit courses, with a minimum of 6 credits. A minimum cumulative GPA of 2.25 must be established and maintained after the first semester/quarter of college. Completed Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) is a requirement. Application Process Review the eligibility criteria to ensure you meet all the requirements. Prepare your scholarship application, including all required documents and the essay. Submit your application by the specified deadline. If approved, submit a thank you letter by the specified deadline. Incomplete applications will not be processed. Late documents or applications will not be accepted. Please allow 6 to 8 weeks AFTER the deadline for applications to be evaluated and updated online. If your application has been approved, your application status will be updated and you will receive a status update email.

MECA Scholarship
Amount:
$1,000
Deadline:
November 1, 2025

The MECA Scholarship was established by the Minnesota Erosion Control Association (MECA) in 2007. MECA wishes to award this $1,000 scholarship to an individual who wants to pursue an engineering or science degree with an emphasis on erosion and sediment control or stormwater management. Scholarship Details $1,000 scholarship awarded annually by the Minnesota Erosion Control Association Open to students pursuing degrees in engineering or science Emphasis on careers in erosion and sediment control or stormwater management Available to new college students and currently enrolled students Applicants may attend any accredited college or university, regardless of location Eligibility Criteria Must be a Minnesota resident Must have a minimum GPA of 2.5 Must be pursuing or planning to pursue an engineering or science degree Must demonstrate interest in a career related to stormwater management or erosion and sediment control Should show evidence of leadership, community involvement, or special achievements Application Process Complete the official MECA Scholarship Application form Write a one-page essay explaining why you deserve the MECA Memorial Scholarship Include your educational, career, and personal goals, along with any special interests or circumstances Describe leadership experiences, community service, and relevant achievements Format the essay in 11-point font with 1-inch margins, using your name and essay title as the header Submit the completed application and essay by mail or email (digital submission preferred) Once awarded, proof of school registration for the upcoming semester is required before release of the scholarship check. You must furnish any other applicable transcripts you would like the Scholarship Committee to consider. All applicants will be notified, by email, of their eligibility and the results of their applications. The MECA Board of Directors reserve the right to abstain from awarding an annual scholarship. The scholarship winner will be invited to attend the Minnesota Erosion Control Association’s annual conference in January.
